About the Coasthopper

The Coasthopper is operated by Sanders Coaches and runs year-round along the North Norfolk coast. There are two routes that together cover the entire coastline from Wells-next-the-Sea in the west to North Walsham in the east — a stretch of around 90 miles. Sanders were named Top Independent Bus Operator at the UK Bus Awards in both 2023 and 2024, and the service genuinely lives up to the reputation — reliable, regular and run by people who clearly love the coast they serve.

For walkers, the Coasthopper is transformative. It turns the Norfolk Coast Path from a demanding multi-day expedition requiring complex logistics into a series of brilliant day walks, each one doable on its own with a straightforward bus connection home. You can park your car once and spend days exploring the coast without moving it.

"Park the car in Cromer, spend three days walking the coast in each direction, sleep in a different village each night, and never worry about getting back. That is what the Coasthopper makes possible."

The Two Routes

CH1 — Wells-next-the-Sea to Cromer

The western and longer of the two routes, CH1 covers the most famous stretch of the North Norfolk coast — the salt marshes, seal colonies and nature reserves between Wells and Cromer. This is the route for big skies, birdwatching and some of the finest coastal scenery in England.

CH2 — Cromer to North Walsham (via the coast)

The eastern route, CH2 follows the coast road from Cromer through the quieter villages of Overstrand, Sidestrand, Trimingham and Mundesley before heading inland to North Walsham. This is the less-visited, less-documented stretch of the Norfolk coast — the Deep History Coast — with dramatic chalk and clay cliffs, quieter beaches and some genuinely hidden walking.

Tickets and Fares

Day Explorer Ticket
All-day unlimited travel
Valid on the entire Sanders network including both CH1 and CH2. The best value option for a full day's walking — hop on and off as many times as you like.
£12 adult
Coastal Explorer Ticket
3-day or 7-day pass
For multi-day walking trips. The 7-day pass works out at roughly £7/day — exceptional value if you're spending a week walking the coast section by section.
£25 (3-day Coastal) / £34 (Weekly Coastal)
Ticket tip

Single fares are capped at £3. Explorer Day and multi-day tickets can be bought online at sanderscoaches.com/coasthopper or from the driver. Dogs travel free and are welcome on all Coasthopper services — though they must not sit on the seats. Bikes can travel if space permits — check with the driver.

Practical Information

Getting there by train
From Norwich: Routes X40/X44 to Cromer or Sheringham, then Coasthopper
From London: Greater Anglia to Norwich, then as above
Bittern Line: Train from Norwich stops at Cromer, Sheringham, West Runton, Weybourne and Holt
North Walsham: Train from Norwich, then CH2 to the coast
Parking if driving
Cromer: Runton Road Car Park on the clifftop — Coasthopper stops outside
Sheringham: Station Approach — large, free on Sundays
Wells: Holkham Road car park, 10 mins walk to Coasthopper stop
North Walsham: Town centre car parks — free after 3pm
Frequency
CH1: Approx every 60 mins in summer, every 90 mins in winter
CH2: Approx every 60 mins daily
Last buses: Check Sanders website — typically 6pm or later in summer
Timetables & tickets: sanderscoaches.com/coasthopper
Good to know
Dogs: Welcome free of charge, not on seats
Bikes: Space permitting, ask driver
Pushchairs: Welcome, folded if possible
Real-time tracking: Sanders app available free

The 8 Coasthopper Walks

We've divided the route into 8 manageable day walks, each with its own dedicated guide. Every walk is designed so you can get off at one stop, walk to the next, and catch the bus back — or walk in the other direction if you prefer. Mix and match to create multi-day itineraries.

🚌 CH1 Walks — Wells to Cromer
CH1 · Walk 1
Wells-next-the-Sea to Blakeney: Salt Marshes and the Stiffkey Coast
Beach huts, ancient pines, purple salt marshes and the harbour at Blakeney. One of the finest coastal walks in Norfolk.
8 miles3–4 hours🟢 Easy
CH1 · Walk 2
Blakeney to Cley to Salthouse: The Heart of the North Norfolk Coast
Blakeney Point views, Cley windmill, reed beds alive with marsh harriers and the shingle bank at Salthouse. The classic Norfolk coast walk.
6 miles2.5–3 hours🟢 Easy
CH1 · Walk 3
Salthouse to Weybourne to Sheringham: Shingle, Heath and Sea
Exposed shingle beach gives way to chalk heath above Weybourne, then the clifftop path into Sheringham with its famous independent shops and cafés.
7 miles3–3.5 hours🟡 Moderate
CH1 · Walk 4
Sheringham to East Runton to Cromer: Cliffs, Crabs and the Victorian Pier
The clifftop path between Sheringham and Cromer is one of the finest short coastal walks in Norfolk. Ends with Cromer's Victorian pier and the county's finest crab.
6 miles2.5–3 hours🟢 Easy
🚌 CH2 Walks — Cromer to North Walsham
CH2 · Walk 5
Cromer to Overstrand: The Clifftop Path South
A short, stunning clifftop walk from Cromer lighthouse to the quiet village of Overstrand — once known as the village of millionaires for its grand Edwardian villas.
3 miles1.5 hours🟢 Easy
CH2 · Walk 6
Overstrand to Sidestrand to Trimingham: The Deep History Cliffs
Some of the most dramatic — and actively eroding — cliffs in Norfolk. Sidestrand's clifftop church is slowly being reclaimed by the sea. A walk unlike anywhere else on the Norfolk coast.
4 miles2 hours🟡 Moderate
CH2 · Walk 7
Trimingham to Mundesley: Clifftop Village to Sandy Beach
The clifftop path descends to Mundesley's wide sandy beach — one of the quietest and most underrated on the Norfolk coast. Finish with tea and cake at the beachfront café.
3 miles1.5 hours🟢 Easy
CH2 · Walk 8 · ★ Local favourite
Mundesley to Walcott: The Coastal Road Walk
The Norfolk Coast Path hugs the clifftop between Mundesley and Walcott — the only point on the Norfolk coast where the road runs right alongside the sea. A short walk with big drama.
3 miles1.5 hours🟢 Easy
Planning a multi-day trip

The Coasthopper makes a 2–3 day walking holiday along the Norfolk coast genuinely easy to arrange. Park at Cromer, walk west along CH1 on day one (Cromer to Sheringham — bus back), walk east along CH2 on day two (Cromer to Mundesley — bus back). On day three, connect both routes for a full day from Wells to Cromer or beyond. The 7-day Coastal Explorer ticket covers everything for around £30.
