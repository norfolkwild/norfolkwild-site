Blakeney Point: The Complete Guide to Norfolk's Most Spectacular Walk

Blakeney, North Norfolk 7 miles return 3–4 hours Easy / Moderate Dogs welcome Free entry

Blakeney Point is one of the finest coastal walks in England — a four-mile shingle spit stretching into the North Sea where hundreds of grey seals haul out year-round. This is the complete guide: the best route, exactly where to park, when to visit for different experiences, and what to bring.

Why Blakeney Point is Special

Blakeney Point is a four-mile shingle spit managed by the National Trust, stretching westward from Cley-next-the-Sea into the North Sea. It is one of the most important nature reserves on the Norfolk coast — home to the largest grey seal colony in England, a nationally significant tern nesting site in summer, and some of the most dramatic coastal scenery anywhere in the country.

Unlike most "must-see" Norfolk destinations, Blakeney Point genuinely delivers. The walk out along the shingle is meditative and wild. The seals are extraordinary close up. The return along the landward edge through the salt marshes is quietly beautiful. It is, simply, one of the best days out Norfolk offers.

"The moment you emerge from the salt marsh path onto the open shingle and see the seal colony ahead — that is one of the finest moments Norfolk offers any walker."

Getting There

By Car

The best starting point is Cley Beach car park (NR25 7RZ) or Morston Quay (NR25 7BH) for a shorter version. Cley Beach car park sits right at the start of the shingle spit and is the classic starting point for the full walk. Parking costs a few pounds — bring change or use the PayByPhone app.

Local tip

Arrive before 9am in summer to get a parking space at Cley Beach. By 10am on a sunny weekend it is completely full. Alternatively, park in Blakeney village and walk the salt marsh path to Morston Quay — it adds a gorgeous 2 miles to the walk and avoids the car park altogether.

The Seals

Grey seals haul out on the sand and shingle around the Point in large numbers year-round. In winter (November–January) it is pupping season — you will see tiny white seal pups being nursed by their mothers. In summer the adults are present in good numbers, lying about with magnificent indifference to passing walkers.

⚠️ Keep your distance: The National Trust recommends staying at least 100 metres from seals at all times. Never approach pups — their mothers are close by and can be protective. Dogs must be on leads around the Point. National Trust wardens are present in season and the rules are there for good reason.

What to Bring

🥾
Waterproof Walking Boots
Essential for the salt marsh return route. The shingle section is fine in trainers but the marsh path gets deep and sticky. Ankle support also helps on the shifting shingle ridge.

🔭
Binoculars
Transforms the birdwatching along Cley Marshes. You will see marsh harriers, avocets and waders in the lagoons even from the shingle path. An 8x42 is ideal for this kind of coastal use.

🧥
Windproof Jacket
The Point is fully exposed to the North Sea. Even on warm days the wind can be fierce. A packable windproof takes up no room and makes a significant difference to the experience.

Nearby: Make a Day of It

  • Cley Windmill — iconic North Norfolk landmark, lovely café inside, 5 minutes from the car park
  • Blakeney village — excellent pubs, the White Horse is outstanding for lunch or dinner
  • Cley Marshes Visitor Centre — Norfolk Wildlife Trust reserve, brilliant for birds
  • Morston Quay boat trips — if you don't want the full walk, National Trust seal boat trips run from here
