Outdoor activities in Norfolk, particularly coastal walking, carry inherent risks. Conditions can change rapidly. Always check tide times, weather forecasts, and local signage before setting out. We accept no liability for any injury, loss, or damage arising from the use of information on this website.

Tidal & Coastal Risks

Many of the walks described on this site, including Blakeney Point and Snettisham, are highly dependent on tide times. Getting cut off by the tide is a real and life-threatening danger. You must consult official tide tables (such as those provided by the BBC or local harbor masters) before attempting these routes.

Terrain & Difficulty

While Norfolk is relatively flat, terrain such as loose shingle (Blakeney Point) and soft mud (The Wash) is physically demanding and can be hazardous. Ensure you have appropriate footwear, sufficient water, and a means of communication. Do not rely solely on mobile phone GPS as signal can be intermittent in remote coastal areas.
