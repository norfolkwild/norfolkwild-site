Step back in time and explore the charming village of Worstead, the historic birthplace of the famous worsted cloth. This gentle walk combines rich history with tranquil Norfolk countryside, including a section of the long-distance Weavers' Way.

Worstead: A Village Forged in Wool

Worstead's name is synonymous with the fine woollen cloth that brought it immense prosperity in the Middle Ages. Flemish weavers, invited by Edward III, settled here in the 12th century, establishing an industry that would make Worstead famous across Europe. As you wander through the village, you'll still see many of the large, spacious houses that once housed the looms, some even retaining their medieval cellars used for storing wool at a cool, even temperature.

The village's wealth is most evident in its magnificent 14th-century church, St Mary's. This impressive 'wool church' boasts stunning architecture and historic features, including a famous painted medieval rood screen. It's a testament to the skilled artisans and prosperous merchants who once called Worstead home.

The Walk: Worstead, Honing Bridge & The Weavers' Way

This 5.6-mile (9.1 km) circular walk begins at St Mary's Church (NR28 9AL) in Worstead. From the village, the route leads east along peaceful footpaths and farm tracks, offering glimpses of classic Norfolk scenery. You'll pass through open fields, often adorned with summer wildflowers like Campion and Hawkweed, before briefly joining the tranquil waters of the old North Walsham and Dilham Canal near Broad Fen, Dilham.

The canal, opened in 1826, was once a vital waterway for transporting goods, though its shallow depths limited traffic to Norfolk wherries. The return journey to Worstead follows a section of the Weavers' Way, a 61-mile long-distance footpath that traces the route of a disused railway line. This path is a haven for wild plants and offers a different perspective on the landscape.

As you re-enter Worstead, you'll once again be greeted by the imposing presence of St Mary's Church, a fitting end to a walk steeped in history and natural beauty.

Practical Information

  • Starting Point: St Mary's Church, Worstead (NR28 9AL). Parking is available on Church Plain.
  • Distance: 5.6 miles (9.1 km)
  • Time: 2-3 hours
  • Difficulty: Easy, with mostly flat terrain on footpaths and farm tracks.
  • Ordnance Survey Map: OS Explorer Map OL40 The Broads. Essential for navigation.
  • Refreshments: The White Lady pub in Worstead is a historic establishment, perfect for a post-walk drink or meal.
Local Tip

Look out for the old weaving houses in Worstead village. Their unique architecture, with large windows and sometimes cellars, tells the story of the village's rich textile heritage.
